# Competitor Analysis: HP EliteBook 840 G9

## Overview
The HP EliteBook 840 G9 is a mainstream business laptop with zero ruggedization. It is sometimes chosen by organizations unaware of the true cost of deploying consumer hardware in field conditions.

## Price Comparison
- Base price: $1,549 (lowest upfront cost)
- Appears to be 53% cheaper than Getac B360 but delivers the worst TCO

## Critical Weaknesses
1. **Zero Ruggedness**: No MIL-STD rating, no IP rating. One drop or spill can destroy the device.
2. **15% Annual Failure Rate in Field Use**: The highest failure rate among all compared devices. In harsh environments, expect to replace the entire fleet within 3-4 years.
3. **1-Year Warranty Only**: After year one, all repairs are out-of-pocket. Extended warranties cost extra and still exclude accidental damage.
4. **No Outdoor Display**: Standard 400 nit display is unusable in direct sunlight.
5. **No Field-Specific Features**: No hot-swap battery, no vehicle dock, no barcode reader, no dedicated GPS, no glove-compatible touchscreen.

## TCO Analysis (100 devices, 5 years)
- Acquisition cost: $154,900 (saves $175,000 vs Getac initially)
- Year 1 failures: ~15 devices x $450 repair = $6,750
- Years 2-5 failures: ~60 more devices (no warranty) = $27,000 in repairs
- Complete replacements needed: ~40 devices over 5 years = $61,960
- Downtime cost: ~75 failures x 16 hours x $50/hour = $60,000
- **5-Year TCO: $310,610 vs Getac B360's ~$350,000**
- **Gap narrows to <$40,000 while providing vastly inferior worker experience**
- Additional hidden costs: worker frustration, data loss, and safety risks

## When HP EliteBook Makes Sense
- Office-only deployments
- No environmental exposure
- Workers never leave temperature-controlled environments
